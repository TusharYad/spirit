Chess

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a chessboard, a checkered gameboard with 64 squares arranged in an 8Ã—8 grid.[1] The game is played by millions of people worldwide.

Rules

There will be 2 groups (A and B) each consisting of 4 teams.

1 team will be of IITG in each group.

Each team will consist of 5 (4+1) players i.e. 4 players will play in a match and 1 is extra.

First there will be a round robin in each group and top 2 teams from each group will qualify for semi-finals.

If there is a tie between 2 teams for a place in semifinal spot then priority order to brake tie is. a. Head to head result will be taken into consideration. b. Sumofindividualpoints. c. Tie breaker match which will be 4 vs 4 with reduced time limit.

If there is a tie between 3 teams, then priority order to break tie is a. Sum of individual points. b. Tie breaker match which will be 4vs4 with reduced time control.

If there is a tie between 4 teams then the priority order for tie break is sub points a and b under point F.

Semifinals match will be A1 vs B2 and B2 vs A1

Semifinals match will be A1 vs B2 and B2 vs A1

Players must give the list of players who are going to play with the board number on which they are going to play only 2 minutes before scheduled time to the arbiter. Teams can change the order of the players in every match according to their wish. Any 4 players among 5 can play in any match

Toss for color will be done on the first board only. Other boards will have the color with respect to first board. For example, if a team has white on board 1 then its color will be black, white, and black on boards no 2, 3, and 4 respectively.

Time control will be 30 minutes + 30 sec increment from move 1.

Time control for tie breakers is 5minutes + 3 sec increment form move 1.
